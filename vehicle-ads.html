The challenge

Automotive advertisers represent one of the largest digital ad spending categories globally. Yet the existing ad formats weren't purpose-built for how consumers actually shop for vehicles — browsing inventory, comparing specs, filtering by price and location.

The opportunity was clear: build a vertically-tailored ad experience that brings the dealership to the search results page — powered by ML models that match high-intent shoppers with the most relevant inventory in real time.

My role & approach

As Global Product Lead, I owned the end-to-end product strategy — from identifying the market opportunity through ML model definition, advertiser experience design, auction mechanics, and go-to-market launch across US and Canada.

I partnered closely with ML research teams to translate ranking and relevance breakthroughs into production-ready features, while collaborating with sales, policy, and engineering to ensure advertiser adoption and policy compliance at scale.

How we built it

1

Market & user research

Deep analysis of automotive advertiser needs and shopper behavior patterns. Identified the gap between high-intent search queries and the generic ad formats being served.

2

ML model & ranking design

Defined the ranking criteria and worked with ML engineers to build relevance models that match shoppers with the most relevant inventory based on intent signals, location, and preferences.

3

Format & UX development

Designed the ad format to surface structured inventory data — pricing, specs, dealer info — directly in search results, reducing friction for shoppers and increasing relevance for advertisers.

4

Launch & scale

Shipped commercially within 6 months. Established frameworks for translating ML research into bidding optimization features, reducing concept-to-launch cycle time by 35%.

Key product pillars

Built for multi-market expansion from day one — launched in US/Canada with a framework designed to scale across geographies and vehicle categories.

Key learnings

The biggest insight from this launch was that speed-to-market matters as much as perfection. By establishing clear frameworks for translating ML research into product features, we cut our concept-to-launch time significantly — shipping a commercially viable product in under six months.

The second learning was about bridging the gap between research and commercialization. The most sophisticated ML model is worthless if it doesn't ship. I focused on creating a repeatable process where research prototypes could be evaluated against commercial criteria early, killing low-potential ideas fast and doubling down on winners.
