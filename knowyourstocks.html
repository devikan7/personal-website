The challenge

Retail investors are at a structural disadvantage. Institutional funds have armies of analysts listening to every earnings call, conducting channel checks with employees, and synthesizing thousands of hours of qualitative data into investment theses. Individual investors get a 2-line summary on a news app.

The insight was that LLMs had become good enough to close this gap — to ingest raw transcripts from earnings calls, 1-on-1 interviews with company employees, and analyst conversations, then produce the kind of deep, nuanced investment analysis that was previously locked behind six-figure Bloomberg terminals.

My role & approach

As founder, I owned the full product vision — from identifying the market gap through designing the AI pipeline architecture, building the ingestion workflow, and crafting the customer-facing analysis experience. The core thesis was simple: if LLMs could read a thousand transcripts and synthesize them the way a top analyst would, retail investors could access institutional-grade intelligence for the first time.

The product has two distinct surfaces: an admin dashboard where transcripts are uploaded, classified, and processed through the AI pipeline — and a customer-facing portal where investors browse stocks and read AI-generated analysis that cites its sources (specific earnings calls, specific interviews) so users can always trace a claim back to its origin.

How it works

1

Ingest & classify

Transcripts from earnings calls, employee 1-on-1s, and analyst conversations are uploaded and automatically classified by type, company, and date. The system extracts speaker segments and metadata for downstream processing.

2

AI analysis pipeline

Each transcript is processed through an LLM pipeline that extracts key themes, sentiment signals, financial metrics, forward guidance, and risk factors. Employee interviews are cross-referenced against official earnings narratives to identify gaps between internal reality and public positioning.

3

Synthesis & scoring

Multi-source analysis is synthesized into a unified investment thesis per company — with a confidence-weighted recommendation (Strong Buy to Sell) backed by evidence from specific transcripts. The system tracks how signals evolve quarter over quarter.

4

Customer intelligence portal

Investors access a clean, research-grade interface showing AI verdicts, key metrics, full analysis with source citations, and portfolio-level recommendations. Every claim is traceable to a specific transcript and timestamp.

Product pillars

🤖

LLM-Powered Analysis

Advanced language models that read thousands of pages of transcripts and extract the same insights a senior analyst would — sentiment shifts, guidance changes, competitive signals, and risk factors.

🔍

Multi-Source Synthesis

Cross-references earnings calls with employee interviews and analyst notes to identify where public narratives diverge from internal signals — the kind of edge that moves portfolios.

📊

Traceable Intelligence

Every AI recommendation cites its sources with links to specific transcripts and timestamps. Investors can always verify the evidence behind a thesis — no black-box recommendations.

⚡

Real-Time Pipeline

New transcripts are processed within hours of upload. The analysis pipeline updates company scores and portfolio recommendations automatically as new data flows in.

Key learnings

The biggest insight was that trust is the product. Investors won't act on AI recommendations they can't verify. Building source traceability into every analysis — linking each claim to a specific transcript, speaker, and timestamp — was the single most important design decision. It turned the product from a novelty into something people would actually use for real portfolio decisions.

The second learning was about the power of alternative data. Employee interviews revealed signals that never appeared in earnings calls — product delays, internal reorganizations, morale shifts. When cross-referenced against management's public statements, these gaps became some of the most valuable analytical signals in the system.
